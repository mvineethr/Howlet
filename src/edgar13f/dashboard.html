ABOUT

EDGAR13F is a free, open-source market terminal in the Bloomberg style. It answers "what are famous investors buying?" using public SEC filings, and adds live-ish quotes, charts, fundamentals, news, and economic data on top - all from free sources, with no API keys and no subscription. It's laid out like a trading terminal: a command line up top, and short "function codes" (like DES or FA) to jump between screens. New here? This page explains every short form and how to drive it. Nothing here is investment advice.

GLOSSARY - WHAT THE SHORT FORMS MEAN

The terminal uses a lot of abbreviations (that's the Bloomberg heritage). Here's what each one stands for and what it actually is, in plain English.

Screen / function codes (type these on the command line)

DESDescription - the single-stock screen: live quote, day & 52-week range, an interactive chart, financials, and who holds it. The main "look up a stock" page.
GPGraph Price - the same screen as DES, just a nudge toward the chart. They're interchangeable here.
FAFinancial Analysis - a company's yearly revenue, profit, earnings per share, assets, and cash flow, straight from its audited SEC annual report.
HDSHolders - which of the famous investors this terminal tracks own a given stock, how many shares, and how big a bet it is for them.
PORTPortfolio - the full list of stocks a given investor reported holding, biggest positions first.
EQSEquity Screener - filter a list of large companies by things like valuation, size, growth, and profitability to find ones that match your rules.
ECOEconomics - big-picture data: interest rates (the Treasury yield curve), inflation and jobs numbers, and the Fed's latest announcements.
PFPortfolio risk - enter stocks you own and see how volatile the mix is, its worst drop, and its risk-adjusted return.
MKTSMarkets - a world snapshot: stock indices, currencies, commodities (oil, gold), interest rates, and crypto.
MYMy tab - your personal page: your watchlist, news about only those names, and their upcoming earnings dates.
NEWSThe full news tab: a general market wire, a Fed/policy feed, and an earnings feed.
HOMEYour customizable dashboard - a blank slate you fill with the blocks you want.
OPTOptions (also OMON, "Options Monitor") - calls and puts for a stock. Experimental; may be unavailable.

Data & finance terms you'll see on the screens

13FA form the biggest investors must file with the SEC every 3 months, listing the US stocks they own. It's the source of the "what is Buffett buying" data - and it's always a bit delayed (see FAQ).
CIKCentral Index Key - the ID number the SEC gives every filer. Berkshire's is 1067983. You can type a CIK anywhere a manager name is accepted.
CUSIPThe 9-character ID code for a specific security. 13F filings identify stocks by CUSIP; the terminal translates these into familiar tickers for you.
Q/QQuarter over quarter - what an investor bought, sold, added to, or trimmed since their previous filing.
TickerA stock's short trading symbol (AAPL = Apple). Any cyan ticker in the terminal is clickable and opens its DES screen.
WT / weightHow big a position is as a percentage of that whole portfolio.
P/EPrice-to-earnings - share price divided by yearly earnings per share; a rough "how expensive is this stock" gauge.
Mkt capMarket capitalization - a company's total value: share price times number of shares.
Net marginProfit as a percentage of revenue - how much of each sales dollar becomes profit.
EPSEarnings per share - company profit divided by its number of shares.
SharpeSharpe ratio - return earned per unit of risk taken. Higher is better; above ~1 is generally considered good.
Max drawdownThe worst peak-to-bottom drop over the period - your biggest paper loss if you'd bought the high.
VolatilityHow much a price bounces around. Higher volatility = bigger swings, more risk.
Yield curveUS government interest rates across different loan lengths (1 month to 30 years), shown on the ECO screen.
FOMCFederal Open Market Committee - the Federal Reserve group that sets US interest rates. Its statements show up on ECO/NEWS.
DEF 14AA company's annual proxy statement - the filing tied to its yearly shareholder meeting.
IV / OIImplied volatility and open interest - options terms (expected swing, and how many contracts exist).

Chart studies (indicators on the DES chart)

SMA / EMASimple / Exponential Moving Average - a smoothed average price line that helps you see the trend through the noise.
RSIRelative Strength Index - a 0-100 momentum gauge; high can mean "overbought," low can mean "oversold."
MACDMoving Average Convergence Divergence - a popular momentum/trend-change indicator built from two moving averages.
BollingerBollinger Bands - a band around the average price that widens when things get volatile.
ORBOpening Range Breakout - the high/low of the first part of the session; trading above or below it is watched as a breakout signal.

YOUR SCREEN, YOUR BLOCKS

HOME is a blank slate. "+ ADD BLOCK" offers: 13F portfolio (pick a manager), 13F Q/Q changes, smart-money consensus, watchlists (create as many named lists as you want), watchlist-scoped news (only your names), watchlist events (earnings dates + analyst view), market news, Fed & policy, Treasury yield curve, and world markets. "+ TAB" creates extra tabs for the less important stuff. Everything is saved in your browser. On the security screen, the ← BACK button returns you to wherever you came from.

CHARTING

The DES chart is KLineChart (Apache-2.0 open source, no paywall, fed by this terminal's own data). Stack as many indicators as you want from the "+ indicator" picker - MA, EMA, Bollinger, SAR, BBI on price; Volume, MACD, RSI, KDJ, OBV, CCI, Williams %R, TRIX, ROC, DMI, Momentum, PSY in their own panes - and use the draw buttons for trend lines, rays, price levels, channels, Fibonacci, and annotations. Indicators stay applied as you switch symbols.

FAQ

Is this real-time?Quotes and charts are live-ish (free feed, may be a little delayed). The 13F holdings are not: investors only report quarterly, up to ~45 days after quarter-end, so "what Buffett owns" is always a few weeks to a few months old.
Why does a stock in a portfolio show no price?A few holdings (foreign-listed names, bonds) can't be matched to a US ticker from the free lookup, so there's no quote to attach. The position itself is still shown.
Why is the first load of a manager or holders slow?The first time, it translates every holding's ID into a ticker through a rate-limited free service. That result is saved on your machine, so it's fast every time after.
Where is my watchlist / layout saved?In your browser only (local storage). Nothing is sent to a server or account. Clearing your browser data resets it.
Do I need any API keys or a login?No. Everything works with zero keys and no account. (FRED is one optional extra macro source that takes a free key - the terminal is fully functional without it.)
Is this investment advice?No. It's an information tool built on public data. Do your own research; nothing here is a recommendation.

TIPS

• Every cyan ticker anywhere in the terminal is clickable and opens DES.
• First load of a manager, HDS, or EQS screen is slow (keyless OpenFIGI/SEC rate limits or a same-day fundamentals fetch); it's disk-cached after that.
• 13F data is quarterly with up to a ~45 day lag - it is never real-time positioning.
• OPT and EVENTS (earnings date, analyst view) use an unofficial Yahoo auth workaround and can go dark if Yahoo changes it - everything else on this terminal is unaffected if that happens.
• EQS is not the full S&P 500 - there's no free, reliable, no-key source for live index membership this project wants to depend on. It's a hand-picked large-cap universe; point the dashboard/MCP tool at your own ticker list for anything else.
